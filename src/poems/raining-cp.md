title: "IT’S RAINING IN CENTRAL PARK"
date: 2025-08-05
draft: false
---

We’re standing on the mound
of a baseball field. Our lips

are curled like hand-cups
ready for the catch,

the exclamation of raindrops
suddenly falling

from the sky. What we want
are the tongues of question marks

unfurling from the mud.
You’re looking at me

looking up trying not
to think

of you. Is baseball
 as romantic as Basho

counting his dew drops?
Does wonder look

like the U.S. without
its full-stops? Are we 

closer together than we
are farther apart? 

The answer is
our shoes are elsewhere

lacing each other
through loops

in the rain
because

where we go
is with our hands.
