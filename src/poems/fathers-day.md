You got a new car: a 20 25 Tacoma. It’s practical you said: 
look at the 6 by 7 truck bed! Sure 23 miles to the gallon
isn’t great but who’s counting? You are I guess when you
laugh and tell me it’s your last car—consider the commute
times the distances you drive now which don't add up
to be much since work is closer to you than I am 
here across the country—I get it, I said, it’s practical

but I liked the Ranger better. I don’t tell you this, how 
it felt inside the cabin, you and I bumping heads
over speed bumps against the steel frame, slow morning
drives to zero period algebra, how real it felt unlike the leather
of Family. The word is what I want to mean, but your mother
tongue is Vietnamese. Instead we circle around the center. 
We drive within the lines watching the odometer
tick, time, you holding the clutch, I gripping the nines:
